# HR Employee Attrition Analysis - Key Insights

## 1. Overall Attrition
The dataset was analyzed to calculate the overall employee attrition rate. This gives a baseline view of how many employees left the company compared with the total workforce.

## 2. Department Attrition
Sales had the highest attrition rate among departments. This suggests that the Sales department may require closer review of workload, targets, compensation, management support, or career development opportunities.

## 3. Overtime And Attrition
Employees who worked overtime had a higher attrition rate than employees who did not work overtime. This may suggest that workload and work-life balance are possible factors affecting employee retention.

## 4. Job Role Attrition
Sales Representative had the highest attrition rate among job roles. This supports the department-level finding that Sales-related roles may face higher retention challenges.

## 5. Income Band Attrition
Low-income employees had the highest attrition rate compared with middle-income and high-income employees. This may suggest that compensation is one possible factor related to employee turnover.

## Summary
The analysis suggests that attrition is higher among Sales employees, Sales Representatives, employees who work overtime, and employees in the low-income group. These areas may be useful starting points for HR retention strategies.
