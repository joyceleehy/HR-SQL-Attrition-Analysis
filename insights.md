# HR Employee Attrition Analysis - Key Insights

## Executive Summary
The overall attrition rate in the dataset is 16.12%. Attrition is highest among employees in Sales-related areas, employees who work overtime, and employees in the low-income group.

This suggests that retention efforts should focus on workload, compensation, and role-specific challenges, especially in Sales.

## 1. Overall Attrition
Total employees analyzed: 1,470

Employees who left: 237

Overall attrition rate: 16.12%

### Insight
The company has a measurable attrition issue that affects around one in six employees in the dataset.

### Business Impact
Employee turnover can increase recruitment cost, training time, workload pressure on remaining employees, and loss of internal knowledge.

### Recommendation
HR should monitor attrition regularly and compare turnover rates across departments, job roles, income groups, and workload indicators.

### Suggested Action
Create a monthly HR attrition report to track attrition trends and flag high-risk employee groups.

## 2. Department Attrition
Sales had the highest attrition rate among departments at 20.63%.

### Insight
Sales employees are leaving at a higher rate than employees in Research & Development and Human Resources.

### Business Impact
High Sales attrition may affect revenue continuity, customer relationships, team morale, and sales target achievement.

### Recommendation
Review Sales workload, sales targets, commission structure, manager support, and career development opportunities.

### Suggested Action
Conduct exit interview analysis for Sales employees and compare reasons for leaving against other departments.

## 3. Overtime And Attrition
Employees who worked overtime had an attrition rate of 30.53%, compared with 10.44% for employees who did not work overtime.

### Insight
Overtime is strongly associated with higher attrition in this dataset.

### Business Impact
If overtime is not managed, the company may face burnout, lower engagement, and higher replacement costs.

### Recommendation
HR and managers should review overtime frequency and identify teams or roles with repeated workload pressure.

### Suggested Action
Create an overtime monitoring process and trigger manager review when employees exceed a defined overtime threshold.

## 4. Job Role Attrition
Sales Representative had the highest attrition rate among job roles at 39.76%.

### Insight
Sales Representative is a high-risk role for employee turnover.

### Business Impact
High turnover in this role can disrupt customer acquisition, increase hiring needs, and reduce sales pipeline consistency.

### Recommendation
Review onboarding, sales training, performance targets, incentive plans, and career progression for Sales Representatives.

### Suggested Action
Create a targeted retention plan for Sales Representatives, including manager check-ins, coaching, and compensation review.

## 5. Income Band Attrition
Low-income employees had the highest attrition rate at 28.61%.

### Insight
Lower-income employees appear more likely to leave compared with middle-income and high-income employees.

### Business Impact
Compensation concerns may contribute to turnover, especially if employees feel pay does not match workload or market expectations.

### Recommendation
Benchmark low-income roles against market pay and review whether high attrition roles have competitive compensation.

### Suggested Action
Prioritize compensation review for low-income employees in Sales and other high-attrition roles.

## Final Recommendation
The company should prioritize retention actions for employees who are in Sales roles, working overtime, and earning lower monthly income. These groups show higher attrition risk and may benefit from targeted workload review, compensation review, career support, and manager intervention.
